Published: 2023-07-26

Bitcoin and the cryptocurrency industry continue to make headlines with various developments and challenges. Here are the key highlights from today’s news:

Former FTX CEO Faces Tightened Bail Conditions Amidst Trial Sam Bankman-Fried, the former CEO of FTX, has had his bail conditions tightened by a US judge following allegations of witness tampering. Bankman-Fried is facing trial over the collapse of FTX, with prosecutors accusing him of stealing billions of dollars in customer funds. This case will be closely watched as a test of the cryptocurrency industry’s legal and regulatory framework.

Cryptocurrencies Recognized as Personal Property in Singapore A judge in Singapore has declared that cryptocurrencies should be considered personal properties, similar to fiat money. This ruling came in a case where a crypto exchange accused a former employee of transferring funds. The decision reflects a growing trend of recognizing digital assets as personal property in major jurisdictions.

Bitcoin Price Falls Below $30,000, But Majority of Holders Remain in Profit The price of Bitcoin has fallen below the $30,000 mark, causing concern among investors. However, data shows that 69% of Bitcoin holders are still in profit, and there has been significant buying activity at this price level. Despite the price dip, Bitcoin has experienced increased network growth and activity, indicating continued interest and adoption.

Bug in AMD CPUs Highlights Importance of Self-Custody in Crypto A new bug called “Zenbleed” has been discovered in specific AMD CPUs, potentially leaking sensitive information, including crypto wallet keys. This vulnerability emphasizes the importance of self-custody in the crypto sector, as crypto wallets installed directly on PCs are vulnerable. Hardware wallets and offline computers remain unaffected by this bug.

Federal Reserve Interest Rate Hike Impacts Bitcoin Price Bitcoin approached the $30,000 mark after the Federal Reserve announced an interest rate hike. The cryptocurrency’s price remained stable ahead of a press conference by Fed Chair Jerome Powell, which is expected to impact risk asset volatility. The rate hike brings benchmark interest rates to their highest level since 2001.

Compound Finance’s COMP Token Shows Signs of Recovery The native token of Compound Finance, COMP, has experienced a significant increase in market price, making it the top gainer of the day. COMP has shown signs of recovery and is currently consolidating around the $69.70 price zone. Traders should consider the upcoming Fed interest rate announcement, which may impact COMP’s price movement.

Canada’s Financial Regulator Proposes Changes to Crypto Asset Guidelines Canada’s financial regulator, the Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions (OSFI), is proposing changes to its capital and liquidity approach to crypto assets. The proposed rules aim to simplify institutions' approach to crypto risks and reflect the evolving risk environment. The guidelines will replace an existing advisory and address the specific needs of the local insurance industry.

Web3 and NFTs Continue to Gain Traction This week’s newsletter highlights the onboarding of millions of users into Web3 through Reddit’s NFT avatars and McDonald’s debut of its first metaverse experience. Professional gamers are also expressing interest in Web3 games, and the creator of Axie Infinity is partnering with NFT collection CyberKongz to develop a new game. Additionally, an NFT image of Ethereum co-founder Vitalik Buterin is being sold, and the popular game Neopets Metaverse is transitioning into a non-crypto game.

In conclusion, the cryptocurrency industry remains dynamic and continues to face both challenges and opportunities. It is important for investors and users to stay informed about the latest developments and trends to navigate this evolving landscape successfully.

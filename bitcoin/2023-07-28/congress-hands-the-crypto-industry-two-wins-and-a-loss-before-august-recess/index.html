Congress hands the crypto industry two wins and a loss before August recess

The US House of Representatives has approved two bills to tackle crypto-currency regulation, amid a row over the role of the industry in the digital currency industry, the US Senate has said, on Tuesday, May 16, 2023, to examine crypto s role in illicit financing and money laundering in its big Defense Bill (Stablecoin). () US lawmakers could be asked to look at the challenges facing the crypto industry ahead of an annual congressional hearing on Capitol Hill on Wednesday, 17 May, and senators are expected to consider their proposals for the first time in more than two decades, as they debate against stablecoin laws. They will be considered by Republicans, Democrats and US congressmen in Washington DC and New York, but it is being given the go-ahead for tightening the security of digital assets - including the use of stablecoins to protect consumers from illegal payments worth $500m (400m) while taking steps to stop the sale of crypto assets before the September recess. A bill is to be passed by the House financial services committee to decide whether it would be used to prevent the cyber-crime industry from closing up shop.

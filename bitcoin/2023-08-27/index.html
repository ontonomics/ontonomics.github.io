Published: 2023-08-27

The world of cryptocurrency is constantly evolving, and today we have some interesting updates on various digital currencies. Let’s dive right in!

Flare (FLR) and Ardor (ARDR) are two cryptocurrencies that have seen slight decreases in value against the US dollar. Flare has a market capitalization of $314.20 million, while Ardor has a market capitalization of $63.53 million. Despite these decreases, both cryptocurrencies have shown overall positive trends over the past week.

Quantstamp (QSP) is another cryptocurrency that has seen a recent increase in trading value against the US dollar. With a market capitalization of $7.40 million, Quantstamp aims to create a scalable and cost-effective audit system for smart contracts on the Ethereum network. The protocol uses SAT solver software to verify solidity programs and rewards users who contribute to the software and find bugs in smart contracts.

Ark (ARK) is a distributed proof-of-stake (dPOS) coin that aims to drive the widespread adoption of cryptocurrency. With a market capitalization of $43.65 million, Ark offers various consumer tools and features to make cryptocurrency more accessible. Investors can purchase Ark by first buying Ethereum or Bitcoin using an exchange that deals in US dollars.

Tether Gold (XAUT) is a stablecoin tied to the value of gold, allowing users to own gold without storage costs or handling inconveniences. With a market capitalization of $92.93 million, Tether Gold has seen a 1.2% increase in value against the US dollar in the last week. Investors can acquire Tether Gold by first purchasing Ethereum or Bitcoin using an exchange that deals in US dollars.

Waves (WAVES) is an open blockchain protocol and development toolset for Web 3.0 applications and decentralized solutions. With a market capitalization of $171.06 million, Waves aims to provide backend support for Web 3.0 services and improve the security, reliability, and speed of IT systems. Investors can purchase Waves on popular exchanges using Ethereum or Bitcoin.

Nervos Network (CKB) is an open-source public blockchain ecosystem that aims to solve challenges faced by other blockchains. With a market capitalization of $114.21 million, Nervos Network’s native token, CKByte, can be used as a store of value and for executing smart contracts. To trade Nervos Network, investors typically need to first buy Bitcoin or Ethereum.

ThetaDrop (TDROP) is a TNT-20 token built on the Theta blockchain that focuses on NFT Liquidity Mining. With a market capitalization of $14.90 million, ThetaDrop tokens are earned by users through purchases on the ThetaDrop NFT Marketplace or through third-party NFT Dapps. Investors can buy ThetaDrop by first purchasing Ethereum or Bitcoin.

Alpha Finance Lab (ALPHA) is a decentralized finance (DeFi) platform that focuses on cross-chain interoperability. With a market capitalization of $81.17 million, ALPHA is used for governance and staking purposes. Investors can acquire ALPHA by first purchasing Ethereum or Bitcoin.

Rally (RLY) is a decentralized network that allows digital creators, influencers, brands, and celebrities to build tokenized communities. With a market capitalization of $37.88 million, Rally offers tools for creators to build virtual economies and engage with their fans. To purchase Rally, investors must first acquire Ethereum or Bitcoin.

EthereumFair (ETF) is a cryptocurrency that has seen fluctuations in trading prices. With a market capitalization of $10.08 million, EthereumFair can be traded on various exchanges using Ethereum or Bitcoin.

H2O DAO (H2O) is a decentralized traffic distribution platform that provides industry information and benefits to its community members. With a market capitalization of $23.10 million, H2O DAO requires investors to purchase Ethereum or Bitcoin first.

Safe (SAFE) is a cryptocurrency that has seen positive performance in the last seven days. With a market capitalization of $124.58 million, Safe can be purchased using Ethereum or Bitcoin.

Beta Finance (BETA) is a permissionless money market for borrowing, lending, and shorting crypto assets. With a market capitalization of $51.31 million, Beta Finance requires investors to first purchase Bitcoin or Ethereum.

Enigma (ENG) is a decentralized data marketplace that prioritizes user privacy. With a market capitalization of $131,704.67, Enigma operates on the Ethereum blockchain as an ERC-20 token.

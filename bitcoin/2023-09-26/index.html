Moving on, we have a case of alleged misconduct in the cryptocurrency industry. Lufkin Advisors, a Florida advisory firm, is facing charges from the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) for failing to inform investors about the loss of access to a cryptocurrency wallet containing at least $10 million. The SEC also alleges that Lufkin Advisors placed investors' money in private funds managed by a separate company, without disclosing the conflict of interest. The SEC is seeking to freeze Lufkin Advisors' assets and is requesting penalties and disgorgement of any ill-gotten gains.

In regulatory news, the US Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) has once again postponed its decision on approving the first US exchange-traded fund (ETF) that directly invests in Bitcoin. The SEC deferred on a filing from 21Shares and ARK Investment Management, giving them until January 10 to make a final decision. This deferral came earlier than expected, with analysts speculating that it may be due to concerns about a government shutdown. Bitcoin’s price remained largely unchanged at $26,161.

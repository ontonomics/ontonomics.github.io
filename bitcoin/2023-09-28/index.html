Bitcoin's Potential to Replace Gold, High Standards in Crypto Sector, and Delayed US Bitcoin ETF Review

Published: 2023-09-28

Bitcoin, the digital currency that has taken the world by storm, continues to make headlines with its potential to revolutionize the financial industry. Today, we bring you the latest news and developments in the world of Bitcoin.

First, we have Mark Yusko, the CEO of Morgan Creek Capital Management, who believes that Bitcoin could replace gold as the currency standard. Yusko has been buying Bitcoin since 2018 and has set a price target of $100,000 by mid-2024 and $300,000 by 2028. He sees the valuation of Bitcoin increasing as it competes with the $5 trillion gold market. Yusko also calls out critics who fail to understand the technology or future use cases for Bitcoin, including renowned investors Warren Buffett and Larry Fink. He believes that once technology advancements like the internet reach critical mass, they cannot be put back in the bottle.

Next, the World Federation of Exchanges (WFE) has recommended that crypto platforms adhere to high standards to protect investors and ensure fair markets. The WFE suggests that platforms strengthen their listing standards, separate market infrastructure functions to avoid conflicts of interest, operate orderly markets with controls against misconduct, and meet best execution requirements. These principles, if applied to the crypto sector, will enable its growth while safeguarding investors and promoting transparency.

In the United States, the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) has further delayed its review of applications for the first Bitcoin exchange-traded fund (ETF). The regulator has postponed decisions on applications from BlackRock, Invesco, Bitwise, Valkyrie, and others. This comes despite the SEC having until mid-October to make a decision. The race for a US spot-Bitcoin ETF is being closely watched, as the first mover is expected to have a significant advantage. Currently, at least 10 firms have applications pending with the SEC. Bitcoin is currently trading around $27,000, with a record high of $69,000 in 2021.

These developments highlight the growing interest and potential of Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies. Bitcoin, often referred to as a cryptocurrency or digital currency, operates on a decentralized network called the blockchain. The blockchain is a revolutionary technology that allows for secure and transparent transactions without the need for intermediaries.

Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies are often described as virtual currencies or digital assets. They utilize cryptography, a method of secure communication, to ensure the integrity and security of transactions. This peer-to-peer system allows for direct transactions between users, eliminating the need for traditional financial institutions.

Bitcoin is often compared to e-cash or electronic cash, as it can be used for online transactions and stored digitally. It can also be used as a token, representing a digital asset or utility within a specific blockchain ecosystem.

As Bitcoin continues to gain traction and recognition, it is important for investors and individuals to stay informed about the latest news and developments in the industry. The potential for Bitcoin to disrupt traditional financial systems and provide financial freedom and inclusivity is immense. However, it is also crucial to approach this new technology with caution and educate oneself about the risks and opportunities involved.

In conclusion, Bitcoin’s potential to replace gold as the currency standard, the call for high standards in the crypto sector, and the delay in the approval of a Bitcoin ETF in the US all highlight the growing importance and potential of this digital currency. As the world continues to embrace the benefits of cryptocurrencies, it is essential for individuals to stay informed and educated about this evolving landscape.

A recent study by researchers from the Complexity Science Hub and the University of Montreal has revealed that cyberattacks on digital financial products have caused at least $30 billion in damages globally. The study analyzed 1,155 criminal incidents in the crypto sector from 2017 to 2022, with the number of reported crimes increasing each year. The researchers found that while the decentralized finance (DeFi) sector had more successful attacks, the damages in the centralized finance (CeFi) sector were much higher. Technical vulnerabilities at the protocol level were the most common cause of attacks in the DeFi sector. The researchers emphasized the need for improved security measures in the DeFi sector, as attackers only need to find one vulnerability while defenders must secure every potential vulnerability. The study also highlighted the difficulty in tracking the money trail in the DeFi sector, prompting the development of the “DeFi Trace” project to automatically trace illegal payment flows.

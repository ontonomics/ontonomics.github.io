Building on Bitcoin in the Age of Web3

Moy s vision of decentralized money and finance resonated with many Bitcoin enthusiasts, yet there was a significant divergence in opinions on how to implement these ideas. Various applications within the decentralized finance (DeFi) industry, such as stablecoins and NFTs, trace their origins back to Bitcoin. However, a growing ideological movement began to foster a cultural shift that discouraged and opposed these projects. Bitcoin s history is filled with experimental endeavors, like Satoshi Dice in 2012 and Ordinals in 2024, which faced criticism for causing spam, pollution, or distracting the Bitcoin community from its core mission. Consequently, the developers, users, and capital that could have contributed to a thriving industry of financial applications on Bitcoin were gradually diverted to other public blockchains. This ideological shift and the resulting criticism of experimental projects have led to a gradual reallocation of resources within the decentralized finance ecosystem. While Bitcoin s foundational vision remains influential, the community s response to certain applications has created a more fragmented landscape, with capital and innovation flowing towards alternative blockchain platforms.

The Art of Uncertainty by David Spiegelhalter review the mathematics of risk

Gerolamo Cardano, an Italian mathematician and gambler, authored Liber de ludo aleae (The Book on Games of Chance) in 1550. This work, which delved into the realm of gambling, was groundbreaking as it was the first to formally analyze dice throws and consider the equal probability of all outcomes. Cardano s insights laid the foundation for the field of probability theory, a discipline that did not exist during his time. Today, Cardano s work is highly regarded by probability theorists, a profession that he arguably helped create. His contributions have significantly influenced the understanding of risk and chance, and his ideas continue to be relevant in various fields, including mathematics, statistics, and decision-making processes. David Spiegelhalter, the Winton professor for the public understanding of risk at Cambridge, highlights the importance of Cardano s work in understanding the implications of equally probable outcomes in games of chance. By establishing a systematic approach to analyzing dice throws and other random events, Cardano s work has paved the way for further developments in probability theory and its applications in modern society. In summary, Gerolamo Cardano s Liber de ludo aleae marked a significant milestone in the history of probability theory. His pioneering analysis of dice throws and the concept of equal probability laid the groundwork for a new profession and has had a lasting impact on various disciplines, making his work an essential reference for understanding risk and chance in the modern world.

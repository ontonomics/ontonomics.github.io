Young aide given peerage by Boris Johnson earned £2m in a year

Ross Kempsell, a political advisor to Boris Johnson, was appointed to the House of Lords at the age of 31, causing surprise and skepticism. Kempsell, a former journalist and the youngest male peer in the Lords, also has connections to Carrie Johnson, the former prime minister s wife. Following his nomination, Kempsell s personal service company, Hyannis Strategy, saw its assets rise from £1 million to £2.06 million within a few months. The increase in assets occurred between July 2022 and October of the previous year, a period coinciding with Johnson s resignation. The accounts filed with Companies House reveal that Kempsell earned £2 million in a year from just three clients, including the former prime minister. The source of this income has raised questions and led to speculation about the nature of Kempsell s work and his relationship with Johnson. Kempsell s rapid ascent to the House of Lords and the substantial income generated from a limited number of clients have sparked controversy and prompted further investigation into the circumstances surrounding his appointment and financial dealings. The situation highlights the need for transparency and scrutiny in the process of peerage nominations and the potential conflicts of interest that may arise.

Screens arent all bad : How FaceTime helps me stay close to my kids

The text explores the dual nature of screens and digital communication platforms, acknowledging their harmful effects while also highlighting their positive impact on personal and professional lives. The author discusses how technology, such as FaceTime, Skype, WhatsApp, and Zoom, has transformed the way we connect with family and friends, especially during times of isolation like the COVID-19 lockdowns. These platforms have made it possible to maintain social bonds and gather information, even when physical distance is a barrier. The author also touches on the concept of the metaverse and the potential for immersive interactions in the future. However, they express concern about the blurring lines between the real and virtual worlds, as well as the potential negative consequences of constant telepresence, such as the erosion of private space and the loss of genuine presence. Despite these drawbacks, the author emphasizes the benefits of connective interfaces, which allow for clearer communication and a sense of closeness in virtual interactions. They also mention the revolutionary impact of real-time connectivity on society, enabling us to experience life in all its complexity. In conclusion, the author encourages readers to appreciate the positive aspects of technology and screens, while being mindful of their potential downsides. They suggest slowing down and cherishing the ability to connect with anyone, anywhere, almost as if in person.

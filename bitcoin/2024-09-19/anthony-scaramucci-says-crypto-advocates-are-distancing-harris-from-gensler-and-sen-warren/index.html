Anthony Scaramucci Says Crypto Advocates Are Distancing Harris from Gensler and Sen . Warren

SkyBridge Capital s Anthony Scaramucci disclosed that some crypto advocates are collaborating with Kamala Harris to distance the Democratic Party from SEC Chair Gary Gensler and anti-crypto senator Elizabeth Warren. Scaramucci believes this collaboration will lead to more positive legislation and a clearer regulatory framework for digital assets. However, Mike Belshe, CEO of BitGo, disagrees, stating that US presidents have historically influenced regulatory agency appointments and Trump has been vocal about his stance on crypto. Jeremy Allaire, CEO of stablecoin issuer Circle, emphasized that crypto is a technology that doesn t care about politics, and policies will change based on societal needs and technology adoption. Belshe agreed with Allaire s view but noted that crypto-related political discussions are still necessary. The panel discussion highlighted differing opinions on the role of politics in crypto regulation and the future of digital assets.

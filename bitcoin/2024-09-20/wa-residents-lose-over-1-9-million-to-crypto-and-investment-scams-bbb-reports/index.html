WA residents lose over $1 . 9 million to crypto and investment scams , BBB reports

A recent investigation by the Better Business Bureau (BBB) has revealed a significant surge in investment scams, with losses from fake investment opportunities increasing nearly six times. The BBB s Cameron Nakashima highlighted that these scams are becoming more prevalent, particularly involving cryptocurrency. Investment scams often take the form of long-term confidence scams, where fraudsters create false relationships with victims, either romantically or as investment mentors, to gain their trust. This tactic has led to a substantial rise in losses. In 2021, the median reported loss was $1,000, but by 2024, it had risen to $6,000. Since the start of the year, the BBB has received 32 investment scam reports from Washington, resulting in over $1.9 million in losses. Most of these cases involve cryptocurrency. One Washington resident reported losing $730,000 to a scam cryptocurrency investment website. Nakashima identified three red flags to watch for: 1. Be cautious of investment gurus or romantic prospects you haven t met in person. Verify their credentials and track record before trusting their advice. 2. Exercise extreme caution with deals involving obscure cryptocurrencies or crypto brokerage websites you have not heard of before. Fraudsters often guide you to invest in fraudulent cryptocurrencies or use fake brokerage accounts. 3. Watch out for too-good-to-be-true offers. If an investment opportunity promises you can t lose money or guarantees unusually high returns, those are red flags. Nakashima advised that if someone identifies an investment scam or loses money to one, they should block and report the scam account. He also stressed the importance of reporting scams to the BBB scam tracker and other agencies such as the FTC and FBI cyber crimes. To avoid these scams, Nakashima recommended educating oneself about what to look out for and only investing using trusted brands and businesses that have been vetted

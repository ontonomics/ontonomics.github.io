FIRS to begin E - invoice system to reform SME operations

The Federal Inland Revenue Service (FIRS) has announced a new development as part of its Digital Transformation Strategy, coinciding with Dr. Zacch Adedeji s first anniversary as Executive Chairman. Aderonke Atoyebi, the technical assistant broadcast media at FIRS, highlighted the E-invoice system s potential to enhance business transactions, particularly for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs). The system aims to validate transactions in real-time, promoting transparency and ease for businesses. The E-invoice system is a component of a broader initiative to improve tax compliance efficiency for all Nigerians. It enables SMEs to manage their invoicing digitally, allowing them to focus on core operations rather than administrative tasks. This digital solution is expected to benefit SMEs, which often face challenges with paperwork and manual processes, by enabling them to concentrate on innovation and growth. The FIRS has also restructured into three operational groups—small/Emerging Taxpayers, Medium Taxpayers, and large taxpayers—to provide tailored support to businesses. This new approach aligns with President Bola Tinubu s Renewed Hope agenda, which aims to create a transparent and efficient tax environment. Adedeji s initiatives reflect a commitment to inclusivity and the use of technology to make tax compliance seamless for every Nigerian. The transformation is expected to boost revenue collection and ensure that funds are available for critical services like infrastructure, healthcare, and education, fostering growth and development across the country.

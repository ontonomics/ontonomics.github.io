Trump says he wont run again if he loses in November

Republican Donald Trump has stated that he will not pursue a fourth consecutive run for the US presidency if he loses the upcoming November 5 election. In an interview with Sharyl Attkisson s Full Measure program, Trump expressed that he sees his potential fourth bid as that will be it, indicating no interest in a fourth term. Trump, who launched his first reelection bid in 2020 and announced his latest bid in November 2022, faces a tight race against Democratic Vice President Kamala Harris. Harris has begun to edge up in nationwide polls, while Trump has faced federal and state criminal charges over efforts to overturn the 2020 election results. Trump has also ventured into several businesses during his latest campaign, including Trump Media, NFTs, and Trump-branded merchandise. Harris has framed the race as a critical moment for US democracy, focusing on issues like family costs and housing. Trump mentioned that a four-year break could have made it easier to decide on allies for a potential White House cabinet, but he also acknowledged the benefit of seeing the challenges faced by his opponents. He stated that it was too early to make any deals for positions in his potential administration.

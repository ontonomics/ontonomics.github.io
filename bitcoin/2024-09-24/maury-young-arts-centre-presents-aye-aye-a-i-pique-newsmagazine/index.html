Maury Young Arts Centre presents Aye Aye A . I . - Pique Newsmagazine

Aye Aye A.I., a play written by Stephen Vogler and directed by Kathy Daniels and Carla Fuhre, premiered at the Flag Stop Theatre and Arts Festival and is now headed to the Maury Young Arts Centre. The play revolves around a struggling theatre company that turns to Open Source s Chat GPT to write a new script, leading to unexpected developments. The cast includes Susan Hutchinson, Andrew Wood, David Francis, and Emma Strong. Vogler was inspired to create the play after observing the ease with which Chat GPT can generate fictional scenarios. Vogler acknowledges the potential of AI to flood the market with mediocre content but believes that true originality will always be the domain of artists. The play explores the idea that AI can be a tool for creativity but cannot replace the artist. The production process was challenging, involving collaboration between the playwright, directors, and actors. The play s premiere at the Flag Stop Theatre, which floats on Alta Lake, was well-received, with two sold-out crowds. Aye Aye A.I. will soon reach a wider audience, with a more focused rendition of the show s dialogue, humor, and plot. The play runs on Sept. 28 at 8 p.m. and tickets are available at https://www.showpass.com/flag-stop-in-the-fall-presents-aye-aye-ai/. In summary, Aye Aye A.I. is a thought-provoking play that examines the role of AI in creative processes while showcasing the talents of its cast and directors. The production has received positive feedback and is set to reach a broader audience in the near future.

Meta emphasizes AI & AR innovations during developer event , as metaverse hype dissipates

Meta has introduced Llama 3.2, the latest version of its open-source large language models, which are multimodal and can respond to both text and images. Although these models are not yet available in the European Union due to new regulatory restrictions on AI, CEO Mark Zuckerberg remains optimistic about resolving these issues. During a keynote in Menlo Park, California, Zuckerberg emphasized Meta s commitment to open sourcing as part of a broader trend that will benefit AI developers and users. He highlighted the company s transformation from a social media company to an AI powerhouse, with Meta AI now having around 500 million users. Other AI-related announcements included AI-generated avatars for creators, automatic dubbing between English and Spanish on Instagram and Facebook Reels, and voices modeled after celebrities. Meta is also releasing upgrades to its AR smart glasses, developed in partnership with Ray-Ban, which will be more attractive and natural for everyday use. Despite the focus on AI and AR, Meta has not abandoned its metaverse business. The company debuted the Quest 3S headset, priced at $299, and the more sophisticated Quest 3, which will cost between $649 and $499. However, experts doubt that VR headsets will go mainstream due to their bulkiness and discomfort for extended use. In conclusion, Meta is investing heavily in AI and AR technologies, with a focus on open sourcing and practical use cases. While the company s metaverse business remains a core focus, the future of VR headsets remains uncertain.

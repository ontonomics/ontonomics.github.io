Protect Yourself : 11 Common Tactics Used In Email Scams

Email scams have become a significant threat in the United States, targeting both individuals and businesses. These scams aim to deceive recipients into providing sensitive information, downloading malware, or transferring money. A notable incident involved the City of Tulsa losing nearly $200,000 due to an email hacking scheme. To combat these threats, it s crucial to recognize common tactics used in email scams. 1. Phishing: Scammers impersonate legitimate organizations to trick recipients into sharing personal information. They use urgent requests, fake login pages, and alarming subject lines. 2. Spear Phishing: This targeted form of phishing involves personalized emails that appear to come from colleagues, bosses, or friends. Scammers may ask for sensitive work-related data or request wire transfers. 3. Business Email Compromise (BEC): Scammers impersonate high-level executives or trusted vendors to deceive employees into transferring funds or providing company information. 4. Fake Invoice Scams: Scammers send fake invoices for services or products never purchased, pressuring recipients to pay without verification. 5. Tech Support Scams: Scammers pose as tech support from well-known companies, claiming there s a problem with the recipient s computer. They may ask for remote access or charge fees for unnecessary fixes. 6. Lottery and Prize Scams: Scammers claim recipients have won a lottery or prize they never entered, asking for payment of processing fees or personal details. 7. Advance-Fee Fraud (Nigerian Prince Scam): Scammers pretend to be foreign dignitaries or government officials needing help to transfer money, asking for bank details or upfront payments. 8. Malware and Ransomware Links/Attachments: Emails contain malicious links or attachments that download malware, giving scammers access to files and personal data. 9.

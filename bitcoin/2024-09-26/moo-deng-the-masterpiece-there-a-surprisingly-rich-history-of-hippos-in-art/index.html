Moo Deng , the Masterpiece ? There a Surprisingly Rich History of Hippos in Art

Moo Deng, a baby pygmy hippo at Thailand s Khao Kheow Open Zoo, has become an internet sensation, with her daily activities being live-streamed globally. The sudden surge in visitors, from 3,000 to 10,000 in a weekend, has led to a new Gen Z trend called Moo Deng makeup. The hippo s popularity is not new, as artists throughout history have been inspired by this mammal, known as the river horse in ancient Greece. Ancient Egypt depicted hippos in tombs, with small ceramic faience figurines often placed in them. These figurines, with intentionally broken legs, were meant to protect the deceased from potential harm while crossing waterways in the afterlife. Peter Paul Rubens painting, The Hippopotamus and Crocodile Hunt (1616), depicts a dramatic altercation between three men and a vicious hippo, showcasing the animal s ruthless nature. In recent decades, the hippo has continued to captivate artists. François-Xavier and Claude Lalanne s bronze monument humorously portrays a hippo with a fully stocked bar, while Jeff Koons s Easyfun mirror hippo abstracts the animal s form while retaining its distinctive silhouette. Beeple, a notable NFT artist, has also embraced the hippo trend, creating an artwork featuring Moo Deng and Pesto the penguin in a battle of the cutie pies. The Khao Kheow Open Zoo s director, Narongwit Chodchoi, has announced plans to patent and trademark Moo Deng s image, raising questions about the hippo s enduring appeal as a source of artistic inspiration. In summary, the internet s fascination with Moo Deng, a baby pygmy hippo, has sparked a new trend and highlighted the hippo s long-standing appeal to artists throughout history.

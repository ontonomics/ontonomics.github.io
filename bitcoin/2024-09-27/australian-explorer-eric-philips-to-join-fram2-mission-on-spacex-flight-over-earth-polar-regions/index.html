Australian explorer Eric Philips to join Fram2 mission on SpaceX flight over Earth polar regions

Eric Philips, an Australian explorer, is set to embark on a history-making space adventure as part of the Fram2 mission. This mission, funded by cryptocurrency entrepreneur Chun Wang, will see a four-person crew orbit the Earth s polar regions aboard SpaceX s Dragon capsule. The mission, which is the first to target these regions, will last three to five days and involve a range of scientific experiments and observations. Philips, who has dreamed of space travel since childhood, will serve as a mission specialist and medic. The other crew members include Norwegian vehicle commander Jannicke Mikkelsen and German co-pilot Rabea Rogge. The team will capture images of unusual light emissions resembling auroras and conduct research on the effects of space flight on the human body, including capturing the first human-X-ray images in space. The Fram2 mission is SpaceX s thirteenth human space flight and the sixth commercial mission involving the Dragon capsule. The launch is scheduled for late December from Cape Canaveral in Florida. Philips, who has experience in polar expeditions, has undergone stringent medical testing and centrifuge training to prepare for the mission. Despite his chronic motion sickness, he performed flawlessly during simulated training. The mission aims to highlight the crew s explorational spirit, bring a sense of wonder and curiosity to the public, and showcase how technology can help push the boundaries of exploration. The team will also study the continuous emissions of green fragments and mauve ribbons, similar to the phenomenon known as STEVE. Overall, the Fram2 mission promises to be a mind-blowingly exciting adventure for the crew, with the potential to advance humanity s understanding of space and its effects on the human body.

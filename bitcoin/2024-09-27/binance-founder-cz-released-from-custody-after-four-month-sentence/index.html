Binance founder CZ released from custody after four - month sentence

Changpeng “CZ” Zhao, the founder of Binance, was released from U.S. custody after serving a four-month sentence in a low-security correctional facility. The sentence resulted from a federal investigation that revealed Binance s failure to prevent widespread criminal activity on its cryptocurrency exchange platform. In response to the Department of Justice s charges, CZ pleaded guilty, paid a $50 million fine, and agreed to never hold an executive position at Binance again. In addition to CZ s sentence, Binance was required to pay a massive $4.3 billion settlement to the U.S. government, marking one of the largest corporate fines in U.S. history. Despite this, CZ still maintains an estimated 90% stake in Binance, as reported by Forbes. This ownership stake has made him the 25th wealthiest person globally, with a net worth of $61 billion. Before his sentencing, CZ announced his upcoming project, Giggle Academy, an education platform that does not generate revenue. With his release, CZ is now free to devote his time to working on Giggle Academy, which is currently hiring.

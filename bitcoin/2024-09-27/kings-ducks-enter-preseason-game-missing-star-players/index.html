Kings , Ducks enter preseason game missing star players

The Kings and Ducks will face off in a preseason Freeway Faceoff on Saturday afternoon, moving from the 5 Freeway to the 10 Freeway at Toyota Arena in Ontario. The Kings, despite being the visitors, will be close to a home game until their delayed Crypto.com Arena opener on October 24 due to building renovations. The Kings power-play units will feature Brandt Clarke and Alex Laferriere, with Quinton Byfield and Warren Foegele on the second unit. Drew Doughty, who is out with a broken left ankle, is expected to undergo surgery. Coach Jim Hiller praised Doughty s leadership and contributions to the team. The Ducks will also be without goaltender John Gibson, who is recovering from an appendectomy. Coach Greg Cronin will have to adjust the team s lineup, with a heavier workload for Lukáš Dostál and gradual inclusion of top-level players like Frank Vatrano and Ryan Strome. The Kings lineup has shown smooth chemistry between Leo Carlsson, Cutter Gauthier, and Alex Killorn, with Carlsson and Gauthier working together effectively on both even strength and power plays. Cronin aims to balance Carlsson s playmaking with more willing shooting to open up passing lanes and diversify the team s offensive strategies. Overall, both teams are adjusting to player absences and lineup changes, with the Kings and Ducks focusing on chemistry and timing to optimize their performance in the preseason faceoff.

Accenture ( NYSE : ACN ) Releases Quarterly Earnings Results , Beats Estimates By $0 . 01 EPS

Accenture, a leading IT services provider, reported a quarterly earnings per share (EPS) of $2.79, surpassing the consensus estimate by $0.01. The company s revenue reached $16.41 billion, slightly above analyst expectations. Accenture s return on equity was 27.48%, and the net margin stood at 10.79%. The firm s quarterly revenue saw a 2.6% increase compared to the same quarter in the previous year. The company updated its Q1 2025 guidance, projecting an EPS between 12.550 and 12.910. Accenture s stock experienced a slight decline during midday trading, closing at $349.70 with 2,575,382 shares traded. The stock has a market cap of $219.15 billion, a price-to-earnings ratio of 32.02, and a PEG ratio of 3.69. In addition to its financial performance, Accenture announced a quarterly dividend of $1.48 per share, an increase from the previous quarter s $1.29. The company s payout ratio is currently at 47.25%. Accenture s Board of Directors initiated a stock repurchase plan, authorizing the company to buy back up to 1.8% of its shares through open market purchases. This move indicates that the company s management believes its stock is undervalued. Insider activity at Accenture includes CFO Kathleen R. Mcclure selling 3,000 shares on July 12th, and insider Ellyn Shook selling 5,000 shares on July 23rd. Over the last quarter, insiders sold 21,421 shares worth $6,912,635. Several equity research analysts have issued reports on Accenture, with BMO Capital Markets, Susquehanna, Mizuho, and Royal Bank of Canada all increasing their price targets. UBS

Meta rethinks smart glasses with Orion

Meta Connect 2024 showcased new hardware and software, including Quest headsets, updates to Meta s Llama AI model, and Ray-Ban Meta smart glasses. The highlight was the unveiling of Orion, a highly advanced AR glasses prototype. Meanwhile, OpenAI experienced significant leadership changes, with CTO Mira Murati, chief research officer Bob McGrew, and research VP Barret Zoph departing the company. In the tech industry, Amazon CEO Andy Jassy announced a return to in-office work for 2025, despite employee dissatisfaction with hybrid work structures. WordPress founder Matt Mullenweg criticized WP Engine, sparking a legal battle. X is modifying its block feature, and RevenueCat acquired Dipsea, an app offering subscriptions to spicy audiobooks. Meta faced a privacy penalty from Ireland s Data Protection Commission, fined $101.5 million for a 2019 breach that exposed Facebook passwords. TechCrunch s Brian Heater tested Plaud s NotePin, a $169 ChatGPT-powered tool for transcribing meetings and taking notes. OpenAI CEO Sam Altman presented a positive update on AI s potential, despite environmental concerns. However, his message may not convince skeptics. Lastly, Isabella Vincenza, an early employee of CloudKitchens, filed a lawsuit alleging wrongful termination, sex discrimination, and a hostile work environment, including retaliation for her pregnancy and maternity leave.

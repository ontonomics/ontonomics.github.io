SEC warns illegal fund managers

The Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) has issued a stern warning to entities involved in illegal fund management activities, urging them to cease immediately or brace themselves for legal action. Dr. Emomotimi Agama, the SEC s Director-General, made this announcement in Abuja ahead of the Commission s World Investor Week (WIW). Dr. Agama emphasized that the SEC would intensify its enforcement mechanisms in collaboration with the Nigerian Police Force and the Federal Ministry of Justice to identify and penalize those engaging in illicit activities within the country s investment climate. He cited Section 13(a) of the Investments and Securities Act 2007 as the legal basis for the SEC s authority to regulate investments and securities in Nigeria. The forthcoming WIW, a global initiative by the International Organisation of Securities Commissions, aims to raise financial literacy among the general public. The 2024 event, scheduled for October 7-14, will focus on technology, digital finance, crypto assets, and sustainable finance. During the WIW, the SEC plans to educate investors and potential investors about investment opportunities, rights, and dispute resolution mechanisms. Dr. Agama highlighted the importance of investor education, as many people are unaware of investment opportunities due to lack of knowledge rather than a lack of interest. To reach a wider audience, the SEC will utilize social media and traditional media channels to inform and enlighten the populace about the investment landscape in Nigeria. The SEC s commitment to protecting and educating investors aims to build confidence in the market and attract more investors to the country s financial sector. In summary, the SEC has warned illegal fund managers to desist or face legal consequences, while also preparing to educate and protect investors during the upcoming World Investor Week. The event will focus on technology, digital finance, crypto assets, and sustainable finance, with the SEC leveraging various media channels to reach out to the public.

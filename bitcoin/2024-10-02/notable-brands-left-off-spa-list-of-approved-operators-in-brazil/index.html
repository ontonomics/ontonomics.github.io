Notable brands left off SPA list of approved operators in Brazil

In September, the Brazilian government issued Normative Ordinance No 1,475, which blocked operators without a betting license from operating until January 2025. The regulated gaming market is set to launch on January 1, 2025. By the deadline, 89 operators had been approved, allowing 193 brands to legally operate in Brazil. Notable brands like Bet365, Betsson, and Aposta Ganha were included, but some significant names in the sector, such as Caixa Loterias, Esportes da Sorte, VaideBet, and Zeroumbet, were not. Caixa Loterias, a federal lottery monopoly, plans to launch a sports betting brand and applied for a license before the deadline. However, it was not included in the approved list, and its sites will be blocked by the National Telecommunications Agency (Anatel) starting October 11. The SPA has not clarified the reasons for exclusion, but it is suspected that Esportes da Sorte s absence is due to a formal error. The excluded operators, Esportes da Sorte, VaideBet, and Zeroumbet, have been linked to illegal gambling and money laundering in Brazil. Esportes da Sorte s owner, Darwin Henrique da Silva Filho, surrendered to the police following allegations of illegal betting and money laundering. José André da Rocha Neto, the owner of VaideBet, and influencer Deolane Bezerra, accused of launching Zeroumbet to launder illegal gambling earnings, were also arrested. Other companies like Stake and Grupo Silvio Santos (GSS) were not included in the approved list. Stake, a crypto gambling specialist, will have to wait until January 2025 to launch its brand due to the ban on cryptocurrency in gambling. GSS, launching its Todos Querem Jogar (TQJ) betting brand, secured a license ahead of the deadline and will gain a license in January.
